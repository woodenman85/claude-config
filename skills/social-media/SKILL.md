# Social Media Skill — Insurance Agent Content

## The golden rule
Educate, don't sell. Every post should give value first. People who learn from you buy from you.

## Content pillars (rotate through these)

| Pillar | % of posts | Examples |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| **Education** | 40% | "3 things your employer's life insurance doesn't cover" |
| **Stories** | 25% | Client wins (anonymized), your own story, why you do this |
| **Trust/Credibility** | 20% | Carrier logos, license info, years in business, reviews |
| **Offers/CTA** | 15% | "Book a free call this week" — never more than 1 in 5 posts |

---

## Facebook — what works for insurance agents

**Best post types:**
- Short story posts (200-400 words) — real client situations (anonymized)
- "Did you know?" educational posts
- Video or photo of you (face posts outperform everything)
- Polls ("Which worry keeps you up at night?")
- Reviews/testimonials (screenshot from Google)

**Best times:** Tuesday-Thursday, 10am-12pm or 6pm-8pm local time

**Facebook post formula:**
```
[Hook — 1 line that stops the scroll]

[Body — 3-5 short paragraphs, each 1-2 sentences max]

[CTA — one clear ask]

[Hashtags — 3-5 max on Facebook]
```

**Hook formulas that work:**
- "Most people don't know this about life insurance."
- "A client called me crying last week. Here's what happened."
- "Your employer's life insurance is probably not enough. Here's why."
- "I turned down a client today. Let me explain."
- "The question I get asked most: [question]"

## Instagram — what works for insurance agents

**Best post types:**
- Carousel posts (swipe-through tips) — highest saves/shares
- Reels (30-60 seconds) — highest reach
- Quote graphics over your photo
- Before/after: family situation before and after coverage

**Best times:** Monday-Friday, 11am-1pm or 7pm-9pm

**Instagram caption formula:**
```
[Hook — first line, no hashtags yet, make them tap "more"]

[Value — the meat of the post]

.
.
.
[CTA]

[Hashtags — 10-20, in first comment or at end]
```

**Best hashtags for insurance agents:**
`#lifeinsurance #termlife #familyprotection #insuranceagent #financialprotection #mortgageprotection #finalexpense #insurancetips #protectyourfamily #retirementplanning #[yourcity]insurance #[yourstate]insurance`

## 30-day content calendar template

**Week 1 — Education**
- Mon: "What's the difference between term and whole life?"
- Wed: "How much life insurance do you actually need?" (link to calculator)
- Fri: Client story (anonymized win)

**Week 2 — Trust**
- Mon: Your story / why you became an agent
- Wed: Carrier spotlight ("We work with Mutual of Omaha because...")
- Fri: Google review screenshot + thank you

**Week 3 — Education**
- Mon: "5 things most people get wrong about life insurance"
- Wed: Product spotlight (mortgage protection, final expense, etc.)
- Fri: FAQ answer ("Can I get coverage if I've been denied before?")

**Week 4 — CTA**
- Mon: Behind the scenes (your office, your process)
- Wed: "This week I have 3 spots open for free consultations"
- Fri: End of month reflection / gratitude post

## Compliance reminders for social media
- ❌ Never promise specific rates ("starting at $X/month") without proper disclosure
- ❌ Never say "guaranteed approval" without qualification
- ❌ Never use "tax-free" without context
- ✅ Use "may," "can," "potential" for benefit claims
- ✅ Include your license number on ads (required in most states)
- ✅ Run anything that mentions rates or guarantees through the compliance skill first

## What to ask Claude

**Generate a week of posts:**
```
Write 5 social media posts for this week — mix of Facebook and Instagram.
My products: [life insurance, mortgage protection, final expense]
My city: [city, state]
This week I want to focus on: [education / trust / CTA]
My tone: [professional and warm / conversational / story-driven]
```

**Write one specific post:**
```
Write a Facebook post about [topic].
Hook them with a story, educate them about [concept], end with a soft CTA to book a free call.
Keep it under 300 words. My name is [name], agency is [agency].
```

**Create a caption for a photo:**
```
I'm posting a photo of [describe photo — me at my desk / with a client / family photo].
Write an Instagram caption that feels personal and connects it to why I do this work.
Include a soft CTA and suggest 10 hashtags.
```

**Write a carousel:**
```
Create a 5-slide Instagram carousel about "[topic]."
Slide 1: Hook/title. Slides 2-4: one tip each. Slide 5: CTA.
Keep each slide to 1-2 sentences max.
```

**Monthly content calendar:**
```
Create a 30-day social media content calendar for an insurance agent in [city].
Products: [list]. Mix of Facebook and Instagram. 
Focus: building trust and educating first-time buyers.
```
