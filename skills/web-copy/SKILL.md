# Web Copy Skill — Insurance Agency Copywriting

## The #1 rule
Write about the client's problem, not your services. Nobody wakes up wanting "life insurance." They wake up worried about leaving their family with debt. Write to that.

## The StoryBrand framework (use this for every page)

Insurance agencies that use this framework convert 2-3x better.

1. **Character** — your client (not you)
2. **Problem** — what they're afraid of or frustrated by
3. **Guide** — you (positioned as the expert, not the hero)
4. **Plan** — your simple process (make it feel easy)
5. **Call to action** — one clear step
6. **Avoid failure** — what happens if they don't act
7. **Success** — what their life looks like after

### Example homepage structure using StoryBrand:
```
HEADLINE (Character + Problem):
"What happens to your family if something happens to you?"

SUB-HEADLINE (Guide):
"We help [City] families get the right life insurance coverage — 
without the confusion or pressure."

PLAN (3 simple steps):
1. Book a free 15-minute call
2. We find the best fit for your budget and situation
3. Your family is protected

CTA: [ Book My Free Call ]

FAILURE:
"Most families are one unexpected event away from financial hardship. 
Don't let yours be one of them."

SUCCESS:
"Sleep easy knowing your family is covered no matter what."
```

## Headline formulas that work for insurance

**Problem-focused:**
- "What happens to your mortgage if you can't work?"
- "One missed paycheck could change everything. Here's the fix."
- "Your family deserves a plan B."

**Benefit-focused:**
- "Peace of mind for [City] families, starting at $X/month"
- "Get covered in 48 hours — no medical exam required"
- "Finally, a life insurance agent who explains it in plain English"

**Curiosity-focused:**
- "Most people in [City] are overpaying for life insurance. Are you?"
- "Your employer's life insurance probably isn't enough. Here's why."

## Copy by product

### Life Insurance
- Lead with: protecting family income, paying off debt, leaving something behind
- Avoid: jargon like "death benefit," "face amount," "premium waiver"
- Use: "monthly payment," "coverage amount," "protection"

### Mortgage Protection
- Lead with: urgency (they just bought a home), the bank doesn't protect them, lenders won't tell them this
- Key message: "Your lender protects the bank. We protect your family."

### Final Expense
- Tone: warm, empathetic, never salesy
- Lead with: peace of mind, not burdening children, dignity
- Avoid: clinical language, pressure
- Key message: "Give your family the gift of a plan"

### IUL / Annuities
- Lead with: concerns about stock market, retirement income, outliving savings
- Compliance note: never guarantee returns — use "potential," "may," "historically"

## About page formula

Most insurance agent "about" pages are boring lists of credentials. Use this instead:

```
[2-3 sentences: why you got into insurance — make it personal]
[1-2 sentences: who you specifically help and where]
[Your approach: how you work with clients differently]
[1 sentence: something human about you — family, local tie, hobby]
[CTA: invite them to meet you]
```
