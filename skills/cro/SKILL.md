# CRO Skill — Conversion Rate Optimization for Insurance Sites

## The one rule
Every page has one job: get the visitor to take ONE action (book a call, fill out a form, or call the phone number). Remove everything that distracts from that.

## Above the fold (what they see without scrolling)

Must have all 5:
1. **Clear headline** — what you do + who you help + where
2. **Sub-headline** — the main benefit or what makes you different
3. **Primary CTA button** — one button, high contrast, action verb
4. **Trust signal** — license number, years in business, or rating
5. **Photo** — your face, not a stock photo. People buy from people.

Example hero layout:
```
[YOUR PHOTO]
Life Insurance for [City] Families
Get the right coverage without overpaying.

[ Book a Free 15-Min Call ]

Licensed in AZ | 200+ Families Protected | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Google Reviews
```

## CTA best practices

**Button copy that converts:**
- ✅ "Book a Free 15-Min Call"
- ✅ "Get My Free Quote"
- ✅ "See My Options"
- ❌ "Submit"
- ❌ "Click Here"
- ❌ "Learn More"

**Button placement:**
- Top of page (in hero)
- After every major section
- Bottom of page
- Sticky mobile bar

**Colors:** High contrast. If your site is dark/navy, use a bright orange or green button. Never use gray.

## Forms — less is more

For a lead capture form, ask ONLY:
- Name
- Phone number (most important — you want to call them)
- Email
- Optional: "What are you interested in?" dropdown

Every extra field drops conversion by ~10%. Don't ask for address, date of birth, or anything else upfront.

## Trust signals (use all of these)

- State insurance license number (required in many states, also builds trust)
- Google review stars + count ("⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ 47 Google Reviews")
- Years in business or policies written ("200+ families protected")
- Your headshot — real photo, not stock
- Carrier logos (if allowed) — shows you work with real companies
- BBB accreditation badge (if applicable)
- "No spam, no pressure" near your form

## Mobile optimization (60%+ of your traffic is mobile)

- Phone number at the very top, clickable: `<a href="tel:+1XXXXXXXXXX">`
- Sticky "Call Now" button on mobile
- Forms work with mobile keyboard (number pad for phone fields)
- Text is readable without zooming (min 16px)
- CTA buttons are finger-friendly (min 44px height)

## Common insurance site conversion killers

- ❌ Generic stock photos (happy families, shaking hands)
- ❌ "Welcome to our website" as the headline
- ❌ Navigation with 8+ items (decision paralysis)
- ❌ No phone number visible above the fold
- ❌ Long paragraph blocks with no white space
- ❌ No social proof anywhere
- ❌ Form asking for too much info upfront
